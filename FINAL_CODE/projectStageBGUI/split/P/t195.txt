When you hear Dungeons & Dragons the first thing that undoubtedly pops into your head is the tabletop RPG. Whilst the original roleplaying game is exceedingly popular - so popular, in fact, that it’s getting another film adaptation and continues to be the most widely-played RPG in existence - there’s plenty of adventure to be found within the lesser-known but numerous Dungeons & Dragons board games that have been released over the past 50 years or so. Some of the earliest examples of D&D board games left a lot to be desired, with most being little more than glorified tutorials for the RPG - such as The New Easy to Master Dungeons & Dragons - or even, Bahamut forbid, trivia games about the fantasy world. After all, who wants to answer questions on what spells you get access to as a third level cleric? Luckily, the situation has improved astronomically since way back when, and now there are a wide variety of excellent D&D spin-offs for fans - and newbies - to dive into. Best Dungeons & Dragons board games Tyrants of the Underdark: A challenging deckbuilding game in which players are cutthroat drow nobles vying for power. Lords of Waterdeep: Dominate the famous city of Waterdeep as power-hungry secret leaders in this strategic board game. Dungeon Mayhem: Battle with other D&D adventurers in this party game featuring ultra-fast card-playing chaos. Dungeons & Dragons: Tomb of Annihilation Board Game: Travel through the haunted swamps of Chult to solve its mysteries in the co-op game. Betrayal at Baldur’s Gate: Explore the city of Baldur’s Gate as doomed adventurers in an unusually brilliant crossover with horror game Betrayal at House on the Hill. Dungeons & Dragons: Attack Wing: A miniatures game based on the Star Wars: X-Wing system that sees players clash as two dragons in aerial combat.
